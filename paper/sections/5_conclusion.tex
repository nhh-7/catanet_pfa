\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We presented \method{}, a lightweight image super-resolution network built around content-aware progressive attention. The proposed \moduleName{} combines image-adaptive routing, progressive focused sparse interaction, and low-to-high multi-level reconstruction in one coherent design. Experiments on five standard benchmarks show that this formulation is especially effective on structure-rich datasets such as Urban100 and Manga109, while preserving the lightweight character of the model.

Two limitations remain. First, the gains are largest on scenes with strong repeated structure, which means the method still benefits most when long-range self-similarity is easy to exploit after routing. Second, the current implementation still relies on fixed subgroup ordering and length rather than a truly irregular sparse kernel. Future work can therefore explore variable-length grouped attention, stronger image-adaptive prototype generation, and richer qualitative analysis of level-wise attention behavior on out-of-distribution textures.
